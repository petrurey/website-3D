2L3Y - Realitate

Year 2026
Type Creative Direction, 3D Design
Tools Blender, Davinci Resolve

Overview

For this project, I conceptualized and directed a full-length music video that blended live-action green screen footage with a reactive 3D environment. The goal was to create a single, cohesive world in Blender where the energy of the music directly influenced the visual elements around the artists.

Technical Breakdown

Recording & Curation

The process started with a two-hour green screen session. Because of the volume of footage, the selection process was much more involved than my previous projects.

I organized the best takes by artist and mapped them to specific sections of the song (intro, verses, and chorus), making selection of usable takes easier during the animation process.

The Audio Visualizer

I wanted a central "living" element in the scene, so I built a custom audio visualizer using a mix of Blender add-ons and Geometry Nodes.

I set up eight cubes to react to different audio frequencies and used Geometry Nodes to replicate them into a "snake-like" pattern that twisted through the air.

I used dynamic lighting and specific camera angles that highlighted the shape’s movement as it pulsed to the beat.

Scene Building & Environment

I built a city skyline using simple geometry in order to keep project polygons low. I added a night-time texture and reflective shader, which gave a realistic "city at night" feel from a distance.

For the main "corner alley" where the artists appear, I added personal touches like photos of the band’s real graffiti and posters from their past shows.

Animation & Problem Solving

This was my largest project to date, involving a massive amount of rendered data and complex scene elements. To manage the workload, I rendered the scenes in layers, disabling any objects that weren't visible in the frame.

I also had a few technical issues, like video-texture lag and "fireflies" (artifacts) in the renders. I solved these by switching to PNG sequences for the footage and tweaking the materials until the lighting was clean.
